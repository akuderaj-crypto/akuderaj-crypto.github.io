Siddheshwar Temple

Dedicated to Lord Siddheshwar, a form of Lord Shiva, the temple is located near the peaceful Siddheshwar Lake in the center of the city[cite: 16]. It is one of the most important spiritual landmarks in Solapur[cite: 15].

The temple becomes especially crowded during the Siddheshwar Yatra festival held every year in January[cite: 17].

Pandharpur

Pandharpur is one of the most sacred pilgrimage towns in Maharashtra and is located about 70 km from Solapur[cite: 19]. It is famous for the Vitthal Rukmini Temple dedicated to Lord Vitthal[cite: 20].

The town becomes a major spiritual center during the Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi festivals[cite: 21].

Akkalkot

Akkalkot is another important spiritual destination located about 40 km from Solapur[cite: 23]. It is famous for the temple of Shri Swami Samarth Maharaj[cite: 24].

The temple atmosphere is calm and peaceful, attracting visitors from all over the country[cite: 26].

Tuljapur

Tuljapur is a renowned pilgrimage center near Solapur, famous for the Tulja Bhavani Temple.

Solapur Fort

Solapur Fort, also called Bhuikot Fort, was built during the Bahmani Sultanate period[cite: 27, 28, 29]. The structure reflects the military architecture of medieval India[cite: 30].
